CAMP NOU

STADIUM

Camp Nou is the iconic home of FC Barcelona, one of the most successful football clubs in the world. Located in Barcelona, Spain, the stadium has a rich history and has witnessed countless memorable moments in football history.

Before the new stadium construcrion, Camp Nou with a seating capacity of over 99,354 was one of the largest stadiums in Europe. Its a must-visit destination for football fans around the world. The stadium is known for its electrifying atmosphere, passionate fans, and world-class football matches. Whether you're a die-hard Barça supporter or a casual football enthusiast, a visit to Camp Nou is an unforgettable experience that captures the essence of the beautiful game.

HISTORY

Inaugurated on September 24, 1957, Camp Nou has been the home of FC Barcelona for over six decades. Designed by architects Francesc Mitjans and Josep Soteras, the stadium initially held 93,053 spectators.

Over the years, it has undergone several renovations and expansions, increasing its capacity to the current 99,354. Camp Nou has hosted numerous iconic events, including the 1982 FIFA World Cup, the 1992 Summer Olympics, and countless memorable football matches. The stadium stands as a symbol of football heritage and the enduring spirit of FC Barcelona.

CAPACITY

Earlier Camp Nou had a current seating capacity of 99,354. Orginally, when it was inaugurated in 1957, the stadium could accommodate 93,053 spectators.

Over the years, various renovations and expansions have taken place to enhance the facilities and increase capacity. Future plans aim to further expand the stadium to approximately 105,000 seats, ensuring it remains one of the premier football venues globally.

NEW STADIUM

The New Camp Nou, part of the Espai Barça project, is an ambitious plan to modernize and expand the current stadium. The redevelopment aims to increase the seating capacity to around 105,000, enhance spectator facilities, and incorporate modern technology and sustainability measures. The project includes a new roof, improved seating arrangements, better accessibility, and state-of-the-art amenities for fans and players.

Construction began in 2023 and is expected to be completed by 2025. The renovation seeks to preserve the historic essence of Camp Nou while providing a world-class experience for future generations.
